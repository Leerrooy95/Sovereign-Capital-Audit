# Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines key terms, acronyms, and concepts used throughout this research.

---

## Regulatory & Government

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CFIUS** | Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States—interagency body reviewing foreign investments for national security concerns |
| **CHIPS Act** | Creating Helpful Incentives to Produce Semiconductors and Science Act (2022)—$280B authorization, $52.7B appropriated for semiconductor manufacturing |
| **DMEA** | Defense Microelectronics Activity—DoD agency managing the Trusted Foundry program |
| **FOCI** | Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence—mitigation measures for foreign-owned defense contractors |
| **FARA** | Foreign Agents Registration Act—requires registration of agents representing foreign governments |
| **31 CFR § 800.307** | Passive investor exemption from CFIUS review for certain LP investments |
| **Category 1A** | Highest security designation for Trusted Foundry suppliers; permits classified manufacturing |
| **TID U.S. Business** | Technology, Infrastructure, or Data business subject to CFIUS jurisdiction |
| **ITAR** | International Traffic in Arms Regulations—controls defense article exports |

## Research Framework Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Mogul-State Model** | Framework describing fusion of private tech wealth with sovereign state authority |
| **Global Vendor-State** | System where private vendors hold operational control of government functions |
| **Geopolitical Energy Hedge** | Transactional exchange of AI access for energy/capital resources |
| **Cleared Path Hypothesis** | Theory that removal of U.S. broker capacity creates exploitable adversary opportunities |
| **Capital Trap** | Self-reinforcing cycle where administrative chaos necessitates foreign capital dependency |
| **Trusted Foundry** | DoD program certifying secure semiconductor manufacturing facilities |
